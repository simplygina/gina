ABOUT ME

In September 2023, I was awarded the prestigious Commonwealth Shared Scholarship to pursue an MSc in Satellite Data Science at the University of Leicester, where I graduated with distinction, ranking among the top 3 of my cohort (2023–24). My expertise spans remote sensing, spatial analysis, machine learning, satellite data processing, Python, R programming, and advanced geospatial analytics, bridging the gap between data science, environmental management, and decision-making.

My research focuses on mapping and monitoring environmental changes, with applications in landfill dynamics, land cover classification, and sustainable urban planning. I have worked extensively with Sentinel-2 and Landsat imagery, applying machine learning to analyze complex geospatial patterns. Prior to my MSc, I earned a BSc in Computer Science, which provided a strong foundation in programming, data analysis, and computational problem-solving.

After my undergraduate studies, I worked as a Technical Support Engineer, where I provided data-driven technical support, troubleshooting complex network issues, and resolving high volumes of customer requests efficiently. I am particularly interested in waste management, climate resilience, and sustainability. I believe in the power of Earth observation and data-driven solutions to drive sustainable cities, climate adaptation, and resource management. My goal is to apply geospatial intelligence to tackle real-world environmental challenges and contribute to impactful, solution-driven projects.
