Data Sources: Sentinel-2 (10m), and Landsat 8 (30m)


Project Objectives:

  • Compare the classification accuracy of Sentinel-2 and Landsat 8 for landfill mapping.
  • Evaluate three machine learning classifiers: Maximum Likelihood (MLC), Random Forest (RF), and Support Vector Machine (SVM).
  • Enhance classification performance by integrating spectral indices (NDVI, NDBI, SAVI) and textural features (GLCM).
  • Detect and quantify landfill expansion trends over 8 years (2015–2023).

Study Area:

Olusosun and Solous III are Lagos two largest active landfill sites, located in highly urbanised zones. Olusosun, in particular, has experienced intense waste inflow and illegal dumping over the past decade, making it a critical site for environmental risk assessment..

Project Methodology:

  • Pre-processed and analysed multi-temporal satellite data using supervised classification techniques..
  • Applied MLC, RF, and SVM algorithms across spectral bands and calculated indices in QGIS and ArcGIS Pro.
  • Conducted accuracy assessments using confusion matrices and Kappa statistics.
  • Introduced GLCM-based contrast and entropy features to improve texture-based classification.

Key FIndings:

  • Sentinel-2 with MLC achieved the highest classification accuracy (84.57% OA, Kappa = 0.82)..
  • Integrating spectral indices and GLCM textures boosted landfill classification accuracy from 76% to 92% (user’s accuracy).

    • Landfill Growth:

  • ○ Olusosun: Expanded by 4.76% between 2015–2023.
  • ○ Solous III: Showed minimal change, likely due to regulatory restrictions and land-use constraints.

Project Conclusion

This project demonstrates how Earth observation and machine learning can transform landfill monitoring in data-scarce urban contexts. By combining high-resolution imagery with advanced classification techniques, this method provides a cost-effective, replicable tool for waste management authorities and urban planners.
