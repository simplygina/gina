Project Description:

Rapid urban expansion is transforming landscapes worldwide, often at the expense of natural ecosystems. This study analyses Land Use and Land Cover (LULC) changes in Makurdi, Benue State, Nigeria, between 2001 and 2020, using remote sensing and satellite imagery. By applying supervised classification techniques on Landsat data, the study maps urban growth patterns and assesses the extent of land transformation.

Project Objectives:

  • To identify and define relevant class labels associated with LULC in the study area, particularly emphasising transitions from non-urban to urban areas.
  • To generate comprehensive LULC maps spanning a period of 20 years (2001 to 2020)
  • To determine the LULC changes within the study area over the designated period.
  • To evaluate the extent of urban expansion over the designated period in the study area. .
  • To conduct an accuracy assessment of the LULC product.

Study Area:

The study is focused on the urban area of Makurdi, the capital city of Benue State, Nigeria..

Project Methodology:

  • Pre-processed and analysed multi-temporal satellite data using supervised classification techniques..
  • Conducted accuracy assessments using confusion matrices and Kappa statistics.

Key FIndings:

  • Built-up areas expanded by 1,246.2 km² between 2001 and 2020.
  • Significant loss of vegetation and barren land due to urbanisation.
  • Potential impact on water resources, biodiversity, and land use planning.

    • Impact & Future Implications:

  • Data-driven Insights to assist urban planners in sustainable development strategies.
  • Demonstrates the power of remote sensing for monitoring urban growth.
  • Highlights the need for data-driven policy-making in land use management.

Project Conclusion

This project demonstrates how Earth observation and machine learning can transform landfill monitoring in data-scarce urban contexts. By combining high-resolution imagery with advanced classification techniques, this method provides a cost-effective, replicable tool for waste management authorities and urban planners.
